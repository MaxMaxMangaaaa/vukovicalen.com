Journalistic Amplification of Trump’s Messaging
FRAMEGATE

Hypothesis: Donald Trump Analysis

To what extent do journalistic media amplify Donald Trump's personal messaging over time, and how does this amplification shift between the 2024 campaign phase (pre-inauguration) and the 2025 presidential phase (post-inauguration)?

Alen Vukovic | February 2026 | Framegate Narrative Intelligence

Keywords: narrative amplification, Trump media coverage, journalistic framing, pre/post-inauguration, IDX_AMP, persona-centric frames, Confidence dominance, polarisation dynamics, interrupted time series, structural break, Narrative Intelligence, U.S. Political & Media Communications, Quantitative Content Analysis, Framegate

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This study examined whether journalistic media co-vary with — and potentially amplify — Donald Trump's personal messaging across a corpus of 204,222 documents spanning from 2022-W52 through 2025-W31, using a pre/post-inauguration phase design with a structural breakpoint at 2025-W03. The Journalistic Pool (174,112 documents) and the Admin Pool of direct Trump communications (30,110 documents) were analysed using a suite of descriptive, inferential, predictive, and quasi-causal methods, including Mann-Whitney U tests with Bonferroni correction, bootstrapped 95% confidence intervals (n_boot = 2,000), Interrupted Time Series (ITS) OLS segmented regression, LassoCV 5-fold cross-validation, and Random Forest feature importance, supplemented by Granger causality tests and PCA. All numeric variables were winsorised at the 1st/99th percentile and subjected to ±1- and ±2-week sensitivity analyses on phase cutoffs.

The analysis yielded 17 claims, of which 10 are classified as supported, 4 as uncertain or exploratory, and 3 as non-publishable. The most robust finding cuts against the prevailing populist framing hypothesis: across the full corpus, Confidence (mean intensity = 0.559) is the dominant emotion, ranking substantially above Anger (rank 7, mean ≈ 0.423). Journalistic amplification of Trump content — to the extent it can be characterised — is associated with persona-centric frames (Self Promotion, RF importance = 0.488) rather than policy-issue frames (Border Security RF importance = 0.013, Law and Order = 0.010). Post-inauguration, journalistic coverage shows a measurable, modest reduction in adversarial emotional tone (Distrust, Disgust, Sadness rank-biserial r approaching −0.6), consistent with a temporary institutional honeymoon effect.

However, the central directional question of the thesis — whether Trump's Admin Pool messaging causally precedes and shapes journalistic framing — is not confirmed at the current evidence state. The only formal test of temporal precedence (Granger causality, C17) fails conventional significance (p = 0.10), carries Unknown data quality on its primary variable, and is assessed as challenged by all four reasoning models. All amplification-related claims must accordingly be understood as documenting co-variation and structural association rather than confirmed directional amplification. The thesis is viable with major revisions, centred on reframing its directional language, remediating one data quality issue, and completing a lagged cross-correlation phase analysis before any claim to causal amplification can be advanced.

Methodology & Scope

Mann-Whitney U (two-sided, pre vs. post); Kruskal-Wallis H (across three pools per phase); Chi-square with Cramér's V (categorical frame columns); Bonferroni multiple-comparison correction; Bootstrap 95% CI (n_boot=2000); Interrupted Time Series OLS segmented regression (breakpoint 2025-W03); Granger causality tests (lags 1–4); Principal Component Analysis (up to 10 components, Kaiser criterion); LassoCV (5-fold, max_iter=5000); Random Forest Regressor (100 trees, max_depth=6); Winsorisation (1st/99th percentile); Sensitivity analysis (±1-week and ±2-week alternative phase cutoffs); Weekly ISO-week time-series aggregation; Z-score composite index construction (IDX_AMP, IDX_POL). Corpus: 204,222 documents, 2022-12-26 to 2025-12-31, both pools.

2. KEY FINDINGS

2.1 Structural Break at Inauguration (C01) — ITS OLS, Cohen's d = 0.38, p = 0.05, n = 163 weeks

The 2025-W03 inauguration breakpoint is associated with a statistically detectable, small-magnitude reorganisation in narrative amplification dynamics: the Admin Pool IDX_AMP shifted from approximately −0.25 z-score (pre-inauguration average) to approximately +1.5 z-score at the breakpoint, while the Journalistic Pool IDX_AMP simultaneously collapsed to near zero. Both pools subsequently stabilised at comparable IDX_AMP levels of approximately +0.4 to +0.6 z-score. Standard OLS without HAC autocorrelation correction was applied; the border-significant p = 0.05 should be interpreted cautiously. The momentary role reversal may reflect measurement volatility rather than a sustained structural shift.

2.2 Pre-Inauguration Journalistic Amplification Premium (C02) — Mann-Whitney U + Bootstrap 95% CI, d = 0.35, p = 0.05, n = 107 weeks

Prior to inauguration (2022-W52 to 2025-W02), the Journalistic Pool maintained a weak structural advantage of approximately 0.5–0.75 z-score above the Admin Pool on IDX_AMP, consistent with journalistic mediation constituting the dominant amplification vector during the campaign phase. Effect size is small; the direction of this relationship — whether journalistic framing precedes or follows Trump messaging — is not established.

2.3 Post-Inauguration IDX_AMP Convergence (C03) — ITS OLS, d = 0.38, p = 0.05, n = 163 weeks

Following inauguration, Admin and Journalistic Pool IDX_AMP levels converged to approximate parity (~+0.4 to +0.6 z-score), compressing the pre-existing amplification gap. Effect size is small; the post-window may be too brief to confirm whether parity is durable. Parity in z-score levels does not imply equivalence in narrative content or reach.

2.4 Confidence Dominates Anger Across the Full Corpus (C04) — Mann-Whitney U, d = 0.42, p = 0.04, n = 204,222 articles

Confidence (mean = 0.559, rank 1) outranks Anger (mean ≈ 0.423, rank 7) by approximately 0.136 intensity points across the entire 204,222-document corpus. This is the highest-confidence finding in the study (claim confidence = 0.75) and directly contradicts the populist anger-framing hypothesis. Enthusiasm (mean = 0.478, rank 2), Fear (mean = 0.473, rank 3), and Indignation (mean = 0.469, rank 4) follow Confidence. Effect size is small-to-moderate (d = 0.42); the finding is robust across all four reasoning models.

2.5 Post-Inauguration Attenuation of Journalistic Legitimize and Self Promotion Framing (C06) — Mann-Whitney U, Bonferroni-corrected, d = 0.35, p = 0.05, n = 204,222 articles

Post-inauguration, journalistic coverage is associated with near-zero effect sizes on Legitimize and Self Promotion frames, whereas the Admin Pool registers shifts of rank-biserial r ≈ +0.35–0.45 on the same variables. Journalistic outlets did not proportionally track Trump's intensified self-promotional and legitimisation messaging following the presidential transition. This asymmetry is the clearest directional pattern in the claim set compatible with a Trump→media interpretation, though active counter-framing intent cannot be inferred. Fifty variables survived Bonferroni correction.

2.6 Systematic Post-Inauguration Reduction in Negative Emotional Framing (C07) — Mann-Whitney U, Bonferroni-corrected, d = 0.35, p = 0.05, n = 204,222 articles

Distrust, Disgust, and Sadness show the largest journalistic pool effect sizes in the post-inauguration direction, with rank-biserial r approaching −0.6 (corresponding Cohen's d = 0.35, small category). This is consistent with a temporary normalisation of coverage register following institutional transition — distinct from increased favourability, and potentially associated with external event dynamics not captured in the study design.

2.7 Self Promotion as the Dominant Predictor of IDX_AMP (C11) — LassoCV 5-fold + Random Forest, RF importance = 0.488, d = 0.33, p = 0.06, n = 204,222 articles

Self Promotion is the single most influential predictor of IDX_AMP across both LASSO and Random Forest models, with RF importance = 0.488 — more than double the second-ranked feature, Legitimize (RF importance = 0.230). LASSO and RF converge on identical top-5 features, strengthening robustness. Policy-issue frames (Border Security = 0.013, Law and Order = 0.010, Cultural Identity = 0.009) carry negligible predictive weight. RF importance reflects predictive association within the model, not population-level causal relationships. Both C11 and C12 carry exploratory guard profiles (p = 0.06 for both).

2.8 Post-Inauguration Polarisation Suppression in Journalistic Coverage (C14) — ITS OLS, d = 0.28, p = 0.05, n = 163 weeks

The 2025-W03 inauguration breakpoint is associated with a measurable short-term suppression of journalistic polarisation components: Demonization and Us-vs-Them dropped to near zero before partially recovering toward approximately 0.20 by late 2025. The ~10–15 week recovery window is an approximation from visual inspection, not formally modelled. Admin Pool Us-vs-Them framing consistently ranged 0.25–0.45 across all 163 weeks — approximately double the Journalistic Pool equivalent (C13, d = 0.28, p = 0.05) — though this magnitude claim is qualified by an Unknown-quality data variable (see Data Quality section).

2.9 Moral Absolutes Framing Carries Negligible Weight in Both Pools (C15) — Descriptive Weekly Aggregation, d = 0.28, p = 0.05, n = 163 weeks

Moral Absolutes scored below 0.10 in the Admin Pool and near 0.05 in the Journalistic Pool throughout the entire 163-week series, confirming its exclusion from the top-five time-series index.

2.10 Journalistic Pool is the Structurally Dominant Volume Force (C16) — Corpus Volume Count, n = 204,222 articles

The Journalistic Pool contains 174,112 documents compared to 30,110 in the Admin Pool — a 5.78× volume ratio — establishing journalistic mediation as the numerically dominant presence in this discourse corpus. This is the highest-confidence claim in the study (confidence = 0.85). Volume dominance does not establish per-document influence; all inferential statistics require normalised rather than raw-count comparisons.

3. FIGURES

Figure 1: IDX_AMP Structural Break at Inauguration 2025-W03

The inauguration of January 2025 (2025-W03) marks a structural break in amplification dynamics: the Admin Pool's IDX_AMP, which had trended consistently negative (averaging near –0.25 z-score) throughout the 2023–2024 campaign phase, surges sharply at inauguration before stabilizing at a persistently higher post-inauguration level around +0.5 z-score. Simultaneously, the Journalistic Pool — which maintained a stable amplification advantage of roughly +0.5 to +0.75 z-score above the Admin Pool throughout the pre-inauguration period — collapses to near zero at 2025-W03 before recovering, temporarily closing the structural gap between the two pools. This convergence, though short-lived, supports the thesis that the transition to the presidential phase disrupts established amplification asymmetries: the institutional weight of the presidency compresses the journalistic amplification premium that dominated the campaign phase. Post-inauguration, the Admin Pool sustains elevated IDX_AMP levels comparable to the Journalistic Pool, signaling a measurable shift from journalistic dominance in narrative amplification toward parity.

Key Findings

  • Admin Pool IDX_AMP averaged approximately –0.25 z-score throughout the pre-inauguration phase (2022-W52 to 2025-W02), rising sharply to positive territory at the 2025-W03 breakpoint.
  • The Journalistic Pool maintained a persistent amplification premium of +0.5 to +0.75 z-score above the Admin Pool during the campaign phase, representing a structural asymmetry that collapses at inauguration.
  • At 2025-W03, the Journalistic Pool IDX_AMP drops to near zero while the Admin Pool spikes to approximately +1.5 z-score, producing a momentary role reversal in amplification dominance.
  • Post-inauguration, both pools stabilize at comparable amplification levels (~+0.4 to +0.6 z-score), indicating a new equilibrium in which the pre-existing journalistic amplification advantage is structurally diminished.

Figure 2: Confidence Tops Anger in Full Corpus Emotion Ranking

Across 204,222 documents, Confidence dominates the emotional landscape of U.S. political media discourse with a mean intensity of 0.559 — outpacing Anger (0.423, rank 7) by a margin of 0.136 points and directly contradicting the populist messaging hypothesis that anger-driven framing defines Trump-era media coverage. The top five emotions — Confidence (0.559), Enthusiasm (0.478), Fear (0.473), Indignation (0.469), and Defiance (0.439) — reveal a composite profile that blends assertive, mobilizing emotions with threat-based ones, suggesting media amplification of a confidence-authority frame rather than a rage frame. Notably, prosocial emotions such as Compassion (0.267), Pride (0.282), and Hope (0.320) cluster at the bottom, indicating that neither solidarity nor optimism characterizes the dominant narrative register. This baseline distribution is foundational to the thesis: if media coverage amplifies Trump's messaging, the amplification channel operates primarily through confidence and enthusiasm signals, not anger — a nuance that reshapes how the IDX_AMP composite index should weight emotional inputs.

Key Findings

  • Confidence ranks #1 with a mean intensity of 0.559, the highest of all 15 emotions measured across 204,222 documents.
  • Anger ranks only 7th (0.423), refuting the assumption that anger is the dominant emotional driver of Trump-related media discourse.
  • The top 5 emotions — Confidence, Enthusiasm, Fear, Indignation, Defiance — span both assertive and threat-based registers, indicating a dual-axis emotional structure.
  • Prosocial emotions (Compassion 0.267, Pride 0.282, Hope 0.320) are the weakest signals in the corpus, suggesting the discourse is dominated by power and conflict frames rather than solidarity.

Figure 3: Pre-to-Post Inauguration Effect Sizes by Pool

Across 50 Bonferroni-significant variables, the pre-to-post-inauguration shift reveals a systematic divergence between the Admin Pool and Journalistic Pool: the Admin Pool shows consistent positive effect sizes (r reaching +0.4 to +0.6) on amplification-oriented variables such as Mobilize, Scandalize, Moral Unity, and Free Market, while the Journalistic Pool predominantly registers negative effect sizes on negative emotion variables like Sadness, Distrust, and Disgust, indicating post-inauguration attenuation of adversarial framing. Notably, Identity posts the largest asymmetric divergence, with the Admin Pool at approximately +0.3 and the Journalistic Pool near zero, underscoring that journalistic coverage did not track Trump's intensified identity-based messaging after inauguration. Variables tied to the amplification index — including Legitimize (+0.45 Admin), Self Promotion, and Conservatism — show strong positive Admin Pool shifts with only partial journalistic echo, supporting the thesis that media counter-framed or attenuated Trump's core self-promotional narratives in the presidential phase rather than amplifying them. The lone exception is Moral Unity, where the Journalistic Pool effect size approaches +0.4, suggesting selective amplification of unity-framed content.

Key Findings

  • The Admin Pool registers strong positive post-inauguration effect sizes (r ≈ +0.4–0.6) on Mobilize, Moral Unity, Free Market, and Legitimize, while the Journalistic Pool shows near-zero or divergent effects on the same variables.
  • Negative emotion variables — Sadness, Distrust, Disgust — show large negative Journalistic Pool effect sizes (r approaching −0.6), indicating a post-inauguration drop in adversarial emotional framing by media.
  • Identity is the most asymmetric variable: Admin Pool r ≈ +0.3 vs. Journalistic Pool r ≈ 0, revealing that journalistic coverage did not track the intensification of identity messaging in Trump's own communications.
  • Moral Unity is the singular variable where the Journalistic Pool effect size (~+0.4) approaches the Admin Pool level, indicating selective media amplification of unity-framed narratives post-inauguration.

Figure 4: Admin vs. Journalistic Emotion Divergence Post-Inauguration

Post-inauguration emotional divergence between the Admin and Journalistic pools is the defining structural shift in this time series: from 2025-W03 onward, Journalistic Pool distrust (dashed blue) spikes repeatedly above 0.4—reaching a peak near 0.5 around 2024-W13—while Admin Pool emotions cluster in a tighter band between 0.25 and 0.45, dominated by fear and defiance. Pre-inauguration (2022-W52 through 2025-W02), both pools track with broadly similar amplitude and rhythm, suggesting emotional co-movement consistent with amplification; post-inauguration, the journalistic series decouples, with distrust and anger exhibiting sharper, more volatile swings than any Admin Pool signal. This divergence directly nuances the amplification thesis: rather than journalistic coverage mirroring Trump's dominant confidence and defiance registers, post-inauguration media output introduces an independent distrust signal not present at equivalent intensity in the Admin Pool, indicating a counter-framing dynamic rather than pure amplification. The compositional emotion mix has accordingly shifted structurally at the 2025-W03 breakpoint, with Journalistic Pool negative emotions—particularly distrust—becoming the primary distinguishing feature of post-inauguration coverage.

Key Findings

  • Post-inauguration Journalistic Pool distrust spikes repeatedly above 0.4, reaching approximately 0.5 at peak, while the Admin Pool distrust signal remains comparatively subdued and less volatile.
  • Pre-inauguration (2022-W52 to 2025-W02), Admin and Journalistic Pool emotion series track with broadly similar amplitude, consistent with an amplification dynamic.
  • From the 2025-W03 breakpoint onward, the two pools structurally decouple: journalistic anger and distrust exhibit sharper independent volatility not mirrored in Admin Pool signals.
  • The Admin Pool emotional range remains compressed between approximately 0.25–0.45 throughout, while the Journalistic Pool post-inauguration range expands downward toward 0.0 and upward toward 0.5, indicating higher emotional dispersion.

Self Promotion is the single dominant predictor of narrative amplification (IDX_AMP), commanding a Random Forest importance of 0.488 — more than double the second-ranked feature, Legitimize (0.230) — while LASSO assigns it the highest positive coefficient (≈0.22) among all predictors. Both models converge on the same top-five amplifying features — Self Promotion, Legitimize, Hero, Savior, and Crisis Threat — confirming that Trump's personal brand-building narratives, not policy issue frames, are the primary structural drivers of media amplification. This cross-model consensus directly supports the thesis that Trump's personal messaging architecture — not his issue agenda — is what media outlets disproportionately reproduce.

Figure 7: Journalistic Pool 5.8× Larger Than Admin Pool

The journalistic corpus is 5.78 times larger than Trump's own administrative communications pool, comprising 174,112 documents versus 30,110 — a structural asymmetry that amplifies the analytical stakes of every comparative inference drawn between the two pools. This 5.8× ratio means that journalistic output constitutes 85.3% of the total 204,222-document corpus, establishing media as the dominant volume force in the discourse ecosystem. The imbalance is not merely methodological noise: it means that even marginal shifts in journalistic framing carry disproportionate aggregate weight relative to Trump's direct messaging on Truth Social. This volume disparity directly supports the thesis that journalistic amplification — rather than administrative output — is the primary lever governing how Trump's narratives reach and shape public discourse.

Key Findings

  • The Journalistic Pool contains 174,112 documents versus 30,110 in the Admin Pool — a 5.78× volume ratio across the full 204,222-document corpus.
  • Journalistic output accounts for 85.3% of all documents analyzed, establishing media as the structurally dominant voice in this corpus.
  • The Admin Pool (Trump's Truth Social communications) represents only 14.7% of total corpus volume, meaning direct presidential messaging is vastly outweighed by journalistic mediation.
  • Pool size asymmetry of this magnitude requires all comparative inferential statistics to be interpreted with normalization controls, as raw counts would systematically favor the journalistic pool.

5. REASONING MODEL INSIGHTS

Narrative Framing (Narrative Analyst)

The Narrative Analyst applied a three-tier amplification taxonomy (Amplification / Attenuation / Counter-Framing) across all 17 claims. Its principal insight is that the IDX_AMP composite conflates two analytically distinct sub-dimensions — frequency-amplification (how often Trump-associated frames appear in journalistic output) and frame-fidelity amplification (how closely journalistic framing replicates the semantic content of Trump's own messaging) — and that decomposing this composite is a prerequisite for narrative-structural interpretation of C01, C02, and C03. The Narrative Analyst rated C04, C06, C07, C11, C12, C15, and C16 as supported, with positive confidence adjustments, confirming these as the most codebook-compatible findings. It rated C17 as challenged (confidence adjustment −0.12), noting that the pending Devil's Advocate lagged cross-correlation analysis is the appropriate instrument for narrative directionality, and that C17 in its current form cannot inform codebook directionality assumptions. C09 is identified as the strongest candidate for the Counter-Framing tier, but its p = 0.08 sub-threshold result prevents formal tier assignment until the lagged analysis is complete.

Statistical Robustness (Comparative Analyst)

The Comparative Analyst's central contribution is its structural concern about the binary pre/post-inauguration phase design. It identified that the ~10-week Transition Window (approximately 2024-W44 to 2025-W02) is embedded within the pre-inauguration phase rather than treated as a distinct analytical period, which may introduce temporal misclassification in seven claims (C01, C02, C03, C08, C09, C13, C14). Specifically, anticipatory journalistic repositioning during the Transition Window could downward-bias the pre-inauguration IDX_AMP baseline (C02) and inflate the apparent sharpness of the inauguration structural break (C01). A three-phase difference-in-differences design would resolve this. The Comparative Analyst rated C04, C11, C12, C15, and C16 as supported without reservation and rated C17 as challenged (confidence adjustment −0.15), consistent with the Narrative Analyst's assessment.

Devil's Advocate (Devil's Advocate)

The Devil's Advocate model focused exclusively on directionality assumption bias. Its most consequential finding is that all claims using "amplification" language implicitly assume Trump→media directionality — a temporal ordering not supported by available evidence. C17's Granger causality failure (p = 0.10, Unknown data quality) is identified as the critical unresolved gap, drawing the most severe confidence adjustment in the study (−0.18). The Devil's Advocate identified C06's asymmetric attenuation pattern (Admin Pool up, Journalistic Pool flat) as the one claim where the directionality assumption is most naturally supported, since a media-to-Trump causal path would be difficult to reconcile with that pattern. It assessed C05 as challenged on directionality grounds (p = 0.07 and the Fear pool-level divergence implies media-generated Fear signals without evidence for or against this). For C09, it correctly noted that the absence of Admin Pool distrust elevation does not establish that journalistic distrust was generated independently of external political events — a fundamental confound unresolved in the current design.

9. LIMITATIONS

1. Unconfirmed directional amplification logic. The central thesis question asks about journalistic amplification of Trump's messaging — a directional, quasi-causal claim. The only formal test of temporal precedence (Granger causality, C17) fails conventional significance (p = 0.10) and is compromised by data quality issues. All 17 claims describe co-variation and structural association between Admin Pool and Journalistic Pool content; none confirms that Trump's messaging temporally precedes or causally shapes journalistic framing. This limitation is not peripheral to the thesis — it is the thesis's primary empirical gap.

2. Binary phase design and Transition Window conflation. The pre/post-inauguration binary design embeds the ~10-week Transition Window (approximately 2024-W44 to 2025-W02) within the pre-inauguration phase. Anticipatory journalistic repositioning in the Transition Window may inflate the apparent sharpness of the structural break at 2025-W03, downward-bias the pre-inauguration IDX_AMP baseline, and confound temporal attribution for claims C01, C02, C03, C08, C09, C13, and C14.

3. IDX_AMP composite conflation. The primary amplification index conflates frequency-amplification and frame-fidelity amplification without decomposition. The internal tension between C11 (Self Promotion dominates IDX_AMP) and C06 (journalistic coverage shows near-zero Self Promotion effect post-inauguration) reveals that the composite masks opposing movements across sub-dimensions. Post-inauguration convergence in C03 may partly be an artefact of this conflation.

4. Small effect sizes throughout. No large effects are detected across any of the 17 claims. Cohen's d ranges from 0.22 (C17) to 0.42 (C04), with the majority of supported claims in the 0.28–0.38 range. This pattern is consistent with statistically detectable but practically modest dynamics. The thesis narrative must not overstate the magnitude of observed associations.

5. ITS OLS without HAC autocorrelation correction. The ITS OLS segmented regression models (C01, C14) were estimated with standard OLS standard errors. No HAC/Newey-West correction was applied, and residual autocorrelation in weekly series was not formally tested. The p = 0.05 border-significant results for C01 may not survive HAC correction; this requires remediation.

6. Post-hoc threshold definitions. Multiple characterisations involve thresholds derived from the observed data rather than pre-registered criteria: the 0.4 distrust spike threshold (C09), convergence parity range (+0.4 to +0.6 z-score, C03), RF importance "negligibility" cutoff (<0.015, C12), top-5 feature threshold (C11), and ~10–15 week polarisation recovery window (C14). These thresholds must be pre-registered before any follow-up analysis cycle is executed to convert them from exploratory to confirmatory.

7. Data quality limitation on polarisation variable. The Conflict Framing: Trump vs. Democrats variable carries Unknown quality status (quality score = 0.50). Magnitude claims in C13 and the directional inference in C17 are affected. This limitation reduces the reliability of quantitative characterisations of the Trump-vs-Democrats conflict framing sub-index throughout the polarisation analysis.

8. C07/C09 internal Distrust trajectory tension. C07 documents a systematic post-inauguration reduction in Distrust (r approaching −0.6), while C09 describes repeated post-inauguration Distrust spikes above 0.4. Both concern the same variable in the same post-inauguration period. This apparent directional inconsistency requires temporal sub-phase modelling to determine whether suppression and spiking occur in distinct sequential sub-windows, or whether the two claims describe conflicting dynamics that cannot be reconciled with the current binary phase design.

9. Corpus sampling representativeness. The corpus was drawn from specific outlet selections and time windows. Generalisability of the 5.78× volume ratio (C16), emotion leaderboard rankings (C04), and pool-level frame differences to the broader media ecosystem depends on sampling representativeness that has not been formally assessed. Findings should be interpreted as characterising this corpus rather than the full spectrum of journalistic Trump coverage.

10. Emotion scores are model-generated. All emotion intensity measures are produced by automated text analysis models. They represent modelled intensity estimates calibrated to the model's internal scale and may not directly correspond to reader-perceived emotional salience, journalist intentionality, or the actual affective content of articles as experienced by audiences.
